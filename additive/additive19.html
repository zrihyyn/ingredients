Process and Prep - Emulsifier, Stabilizer, Thickening Agent

Description

Chlorophyll, vital for plant photosynthesis, is extracted from grass, nettles, or spinach to create a blue-black food pigment. Discovered in 1817, its use in food was limited until the late 1990s due to stability issues. A unique green sea slug uses chlorophyll for photosynthesis. Derived from chlorophyll, Chlorophyllin or Natural Green #3 is used in products like absinthe and as a health supplement, though its benefits are best obtained from consuming green vegetables like spinach.
