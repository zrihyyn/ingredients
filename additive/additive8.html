Azodicarbonamide (ADA)

123-77-3

Synonyms or siblings

Azobisformamide, Azodicarboxamide, Azodicaroxylic acid diamide

Funtion

Process and Prep - Emulsifier, Leavening Agent, Bleaching Agent

Description

Azodicarbonamide (ADA) is a common additive in flour, enhancing bread quality by improving dough strength and gas retention. It originated in China in 1962 and is used widely in mass-produced breads despite being banned in Europe and Australia due to its industrial applications. The FDA considers it safe for food use, as it breaks down harmlessly and is strictly regulated.
