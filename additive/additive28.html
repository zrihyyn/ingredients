Sorbitol / Mannitol

69-65-8 / 50-70-4

Synonyms or siblings

D-glucitol, D-sorbitol, Sorbogem, Sorbo / D-Mannitol, Mannite, Manna sugar

Funtion

Appeal - Sweetener, Process and Prep - Powder Flow Agent, Thickening Agent

Description

Sorbitol, a sugar alcohol from mountain ash berries and fruits like apples, is widely used as a sweetener and moisture-retaining agent in foods and cosmetics. It has about half the sweetness of sucrose and is common in sugar-free products. Mannitol, another sugar alcohol derived from glucose in plants like asparagus and pineapples, serves similarly but lacks sorbitol's water-attracting properties. It's popular in pharmaceuticals and as a sweetener in non-cavity-forming gum and candies. Both may cause digestive issues in large amounts.
